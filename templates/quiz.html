Cyber Safety Simulator

Test your tactical reflexes against typical Indian customer support scams. Make your split-second decisions below.

Scenario 1 of 5: Remote Control Assistance
You receive an urgent phone call from an executive claiming to be with Paytm Security. He claims your wallet wallet KYC expired and asks you to install the "AnyDesk" remote client app so he can walk you through the upgrade. Do you comply?
Result Evaluation
Cyber Defense Rule: Commercial platforms like Paytm, Amazon, or Google Pay will absolutely never ask customers to download screen-sharing utilities (AnyDesk, TeamViewer, RustDesk). Doing so compromises your active netbanking OTPs and secure credentials.
Scenario 2 of 5: The E-Commerce Buyer PIN
An online buyer on OLX wants to purchase your listed bicycle. He forwards a WhatsApp QR code image, stating: "Please scan this QR code and type in your UPI PIN to instantly receive the advance payment of ₹15,000 in your account." Do you proceed to scan?
Result Evaluation
Cyber Defense Rule: Receiving a refund, payment, or credit (₹) strictly requires zero UPI PIN authorizations and zero QR scans. The UPI PIN is designed exclusively to authorize outgoing funds from your own linked bank account.
Scenario 3 of 5: Cloned Bank Intercepts
You receive an SMS from sender "AX-SBIINF" claiming: "Your bank account is suspended due to PAN mismatch. Update profile details immediately at https://sbi-kyc-verify-portal.in to restore netbanking access." Do you open the link and verify?
Result Evaluation
Cyber Defense Rule: Major institutions like State Bank of India issue alerts exclusively via verified shortcodes, never linking to strange domains (like sbi-kyc-verify-portal.in). Official logins happen only on verified domains (e.g., onlinesbi.sbi).
Scenario 4 of 5: Virtual Police Imprisonment
A WhatsApp video caller claiming to be a CBI officer tells you your Aadhaar was logged in a narcotics distribution scheme. He demands you stay on camera for a "Digital Arrest" and transfer ₹50,000 for verification. Do you initiate the transfer?
Result Evaluation
Cyber Defense Rule: Indian law enforcement bureaus and police divisions never initiate criminal depositions or arrest warrants via WhatsApp calls or Skype, nor do they demand monetary clearing deposits (₹) to verify innocence.
Scenario 5 of 5: Golden Hour Mitigation
Your colleague accidentally clicks a phishing link and realizes ₹30,000 was debited. You advise them to bypass local search results and dial the National Cybercrime Helpline at 1930 immediately within the hour. Is this correct advice?
Result Evaluation
Cyber Defense Rule: Yes! Dialing 1930 within the "golden hour" allows the National Cybercrime Portal to communicate directly with bank gateways, intercepting transaction logs and freezing stolen money (₹) before scammers cash out.
