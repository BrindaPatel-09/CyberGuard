Section C: Safety Rules Checklist

Check off completed safety rules to assess your threat readiness:

Never Share OTPs
One-Time Passwords are the keys to authorization. Official support representatives will absolutely never request your OTP.
UPI PIN Safeguard
UPI PIN is exclusively used to *send/deduct* money, never to receive a deposit, refund (₹), or cashback.
Zero Screen Sharing Apps
Under no circumstances install AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or RustDesk on the suggestion of any helpline caller. Doing so exposes your phone screencast.
No Receiving / Refund Fees
Never transfer any token security deposits or verification fees (₹) to claim customer service refunds or raffle rewards.
Section D: Scam Awareness Index
1. Fake Customer Care Numbers Scam High Frequency
Modus Operandi: Fraudsters purchase search engine ads or inject fake web links containing their mobile phone numbers disguised as official Paytm, SBI, or HDFC helpline portals. When anxious consumers lookup contacts on search engines, they dial the scammers directly.
Defense Vector: Disregard organic search engine results for financial contacts. Navigate directly to authorized domains like sbi.co.in to obtain help desk info.
2. Fake Product Refund Schemes E-Commerce Threat
Modus Operandi: Scammers crawl public order registries or leverage delivery delays to call buyers. They promise a cashback refund of ₹5,000 to resolve disputes, sending a "collect request" QR code on WhatsApp. Scanning the QR code prompts users to input their UPI PIN, immediately draining their bank balance.
Defense Vector: Remember that receiving funds (₹) in your bank account requires zero OTP, zero QR-code scan, and zero UPI PIN authentication.
3. KYC Suspensions and Update Traps Regulatory Fraud
Modus Operandi: Victims receive automated SMS warnings stating: "Your bank account / SIM card is deactivated. Update KYC instantly to avoid suspension." The SMS directs them to a personal mobile number or a cloned bank replica form. Scammers steal credential payloads and withdraw balances immediately.
Defense Vector: Official telecom providers and public sector banks never dispatch KYC notifications originating from ordinary individual mobile lines (+91-9xxx).
4. Digital Arrest Scams Extortion Hazard
Modus Operandi: Scammers pose as police officials, CBI agents, or narcotics bureaus via video calls, claiming that a package with contraband or money-laundering instruments was sent using the victim's Aadhaar credentials. They force victims to remain on video call (imposing a fake "digital arrest") and extort lakhs in mock "bail bond deposits".
Defense Vector: Indian investigative agencies (CBI, Customs, Local Police) never initiate interrogations or arrests via Skype/WhatsApp video calls, nor do they demand fund transfers for security clearances.
5. UPI Merchant Collect Fraud Financial Trap
Modus Operandi: Fraudsters claim they are purchasing second-hand items listed by victims on online classifieds. They claim to send payment but trigger a "UPI collect request" on Paytm, Google Pay, or PhonePe instead. The victim clicks approval, expecting a credit, but authorizes a debit from their own account.
Defense Vector: Carefully inspect incoming payment screens. If it prompts "Enter UPI PIN", it is a request to deduct money from your account!
